How we met

We met through a friend Grace Balogun at Foursquare Camp Ajebo.
I was posted to serve at Ekiti at the time and I needed to link up with friends that were serving in the same state. I spoke to Grace about it and she linked us up.We became friends during our service year, and the relationship started to unfold.

Ireti and I got to know each other in 2017, we met through a mutual friend a day after my convocation ceremony at McPherson University. We got talking almost immediately because we were both posted to the same state for our National Youth Service. We were quite close all through our service year as we had so many things in common.
In 2019, we started our relationship officially and now we’ve made a decision to get married. The best decision of our lives.

Events

Friday, 6th August

Church Service 10AM - 12PM

The christian service will be held at Foursquare Church, Olambe, Ogun State. The service will be in the presence of God, close family members, friends and church members. The joining is significant as we are both christians and believe in the utmost importance of joining before the body of Christ (The Church).

Saturday, 7th August

Traditional Engagement & Reception 10AM-6PM

This is the main ceremony where the traditional wedding rites according to the Yoruba culture is carried out. The dowry of the bride is paid and the family of the bride officially blesses the union and hands over their daughter to the care of the groom and his family. The union is blessed by family members from both sides. Food, drinks and dance follows immediately after the engagement ceremony
